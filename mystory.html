Serious Cycling


I started cycle racing in 1981 at the age of 17. I was riding for the Yorkshire Clarion cycling club (now defunct). I discovered that i had quite a natural ability and within a couple of years i was winning races.


I became quite well know in the north of England, settling club records: 10 miles 19 minutes and 54 seconds, 25 miles 52 minutes and 17 seconds and 50 miles 1 hour 45 minutes and 41 seconds
Over the 30 years that iv'e been cycling i have rode for a few different cycling clubs: Yorkshire Clarion C&AC (defunct) 1981-83 Airedale Olympic CC (still going strong) 1884-1987 then 1994-present Knaresborough Racing Team (defunct) 1988-1990 Morley CC 1991-1993 (also defunct)â€‹ In 1992 I won the Post office Nation 25 mile time trial in 52.17 Even though I had a natural ability I never really reached my potential in road racing as I didn't have the mental aggressive edge that a good road rider needs, even though I did manage to win a road race in 1983. I retired from cycling racing in 2000 after winning the CTC (Cyclists Touring Club, Now "Cycling UK") National 25 mile time trail in 53 minutes and 45 seconds.

Short running career


Even though I'd given up cycle racing, I was still cycling for fun on and off, but being an athlete, I had to find something to replace that buzz. That's when I started running and running races. Over the next 10 years my running was an on-off affair continually plagued with injury. Even though I managed a few good results, 50th place in Leeds half marathon (1.24.00) in 2003 and a 35 minute 10k the same year.

2011- The year that changed eveything.


That year started well, I was cycling to work and back. I was working as a postman and keeping quite fit. I was also continuing to run but I'd given up running races a few years previous due to injuries. By June I decided to start training with a running club, I did a couple of runs with them and was intending to do more but all that was devastatingly taken away from me. In June I was cycling to work one morning when I was knocked off my bike by a hit and run driver who were never caught as they had false plates. My injuries were severe with smashed hip socket,fractured pelvis and broken finger. I'm glad I was wearing a helmet. I was in hospital for 2 weeks and had reconstruction surgery. I was on crutches for 6 weeks. By November I was cycling again but only short distances as I was still feeling pain. I went back to work as a postman on light duties. By April 2012 I was delivering letters again and cycling occasionally but my pain was getting worse. The pain became so bad that I had to give up work and cycling. It turned out I had Vascular necroses, where the bone in my hip joint was dying due to no blood circulation as a result of damage from the accident. In September I had to have a total hip replacement and bone graft in my left hip. I was advised by my surgeon to give up cycling as any fall could have catastrophic results and advised I should take up indoor cycling. Ever since then all I've ever done is "JUST INDOOR CYCLING".

My Family


I am married with 3 kids: Elizabeth, 12, George, 6 and Alasdair, 24 (from previous marriage). My wife Maka is from Georgia (a post soviet state). We try and get to Georgia every couple of years to see family. I even have a spin bike there. I work as a bus driver so with the long hours indoor cycling suits me better. I am 56 years old so have my last two children later in life, so anyone like myself struggling with work and family life is another plus for "just indoor cycling".
